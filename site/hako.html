    Hako is a 2 players dice game.

    The game was originally played by the Iridi people in Wiktopher. Hako is played with 8 dice, where each face is painted as 4 triangles with 2 colors. A paper hako dice can be made from a sonobe 6. The Iridi die is a painted stone.

    There are 6 different sides to a hako die. The fully clear side is called day, the fully painted is called night, the side with two connected triangles is called dusk, the side with two disconnected triangle is called dawn, the remaining sides are called null-moon or full-moon.

    How to Play

    Players each pick a color, and begin with 4 dice.

    Each turn, a player rolls a die and try to surround a die in play, with 4 triangles of its own color. After playing their last die, players must pick dice in play which were not played last, and which do not create a floating die. The game ends when a player manages to surround a die. A floating die is one with no immediate non-diagonal neighbor.

    In this example game, the player with the unpainted triangles wins.

    The name hako is the hexadecimal number 18, in Bibi Binary, which is the total number of faces on dice in the Hako game.
