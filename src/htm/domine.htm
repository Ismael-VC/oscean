Domine is a 2-players domino game.

The goal is to have the most “living” dominoes at the end of the round.

Rules

Each player draws a hand of seven dominoes and keeps them hidden from their opponent. The two numbers on either side of a domino are the Attack and Defense points, facing the opponent and the player respectively. The dominoes can be flipped as long as it’s before they are played. Each turn consists choosing which is the attack side and which is the defense side. Players alternate turns until all dominoes have been played. The player with the most living dominoes wins.

The bottom player plays 4a1d, attacking the top player by 4d. The top player defends with 3a5d. The attack is blocked with a riposte of 3a, winning over 4a1d. The top player wins this round, the bottom player flips their domino.

When a player attacks with a domino, the opponent must defend with a value equal to or higher than the attack. If the opponent’s domino defense value is lower than the attack, the domino is killed. If the opponent’s domino defense value is equal to or higher, then the domino survives.

Riposte

If the opponent survives and has an attack number higher than the player’s domino defense value, the opponent can counter to kill the player’s domino.
