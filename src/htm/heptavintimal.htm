In base 27, triplets of ternary digits, also called trybbles, are encoded by single heptavintimal digits.

The name heptavintimal is composed of the Greek prefix hepta, meaning seven, followed by the Latin root vinti meaning twenty, with the suffix mal added, to indicate that it is a number base. The mixing of Greek and Latin exactly follows the formation of the word hexadecimal, where the prefix hexi comes from Greek and the root deci is from the Latin.

The choice of characters used to represent digits above 9 is not a great problem with hexadecimal, since the letters A through F cannot be easily confused with digits. In the case of Heptavintimal, however, the letters I an O pose challenges because they are easily confused with the digits 1 and 0. These and other potentially confusing letters are therefore eliminated from the set of heptavintimal digits.
